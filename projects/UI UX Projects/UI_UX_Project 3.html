BookNest: A Cozy Digital Library Experience

BookNest is a mobile library application designed to create a cozy and engaging reading environment for book lovers. The app enables users to read, share, and recommend books effortlessly while fostering a sense of community among readers. The project focused on crafting a user-friendly, visually soothing interface that simplifies book discovery and enhances reading engagement through personalized recommendations and social interactions.

Problem Statement

In today’s digital reading landscape, readers often face fragmented and impersonal experiences. Many reading apps are either overly commercialized or too minimal, missing the warmth and community of a real library. Users struggle with cluttered interfaces, unreliable recommendation systems, and limited opportunities for genuine social connection. At the same time, book lovers grapple with busy schedules, constant digital distractions, and an overwhelming abundance of choices. Traditional bookstores and libraries rarely offer the personalized guidance or interactive features that make reading more engaging and meaningful. For new readers—or anyone seeking trusted recommendations from friends and fellow enthusiasts—discovering the right book can feel like a challenge rather than a joy. The goal, therefore, was to design an app that feels warm, intuitive, and community-driven—one that transforms reading from a solitary activity into a shared, enriching experience.

Opportunity

While analyzing other library and book-reading apps such as Goodreads, Libby, and Wattpad, we observed several gaps:

  • Overwhelming interfaces and poor hierarchy of information.
  • Weak integration of social features like book clubs or peer recommendations.
  • Limited personalization and context-based book suggestions.
  • Lack of visual warmth or emotional connection with users.

These insights presented an opportunity to design BookNest — an app that merges the simplicity of digital reading with the comfort of a cozy, community-driven space.

Project Goals

  • Simplify the user experience of discovering, reading, and sharing books.
  • Build an emotionally warm, cohesive visual identity for digital reading.
  • Encourage social interactions through book clubs, shared shelves, and recommendations.
  • Implement smart, personalized book suggestions based on user behavior.
  • Create a seamless user journey with minimal friction from discovery to reading.

User-Research

Research Methods:

  • Surveys: Conducted with 80 participants to understand reading habits and digital behaviors.
  • User Interviews: 12 in-depth interviews with avid readers aged 18–45.
  • Usability Testing: Two rounds of prototype testing with 10 users per round.

Key Insights:

  • Users want aesthetic and peaceful interfaces that reduce visual fatigue.
  • Personalized recommendations and reading streaks motivate engagement.
  • Many readers miss the community aspect—discussing or recommending books to friends.
  • 70% of participants said they’d use an app that combines reading with social connection.

A visually soothing, community-oriented platform combining the functionality of Libby, the engagement of Goodreads, and the modern UI simplicity of Blinkist.

Reflection

Designing BookNest was an exploration into how digital experiences can recreate the warmth, comfort, and sense of discovery found in a physical reading environment. The challenge was to merge functionality with feeling — to make reading and organizing books online not just efficient, but emotionally fulfilling.

Throughout the process, I learned how deeply visual and interaction design choices influence emotional engagement. Early prototypes focused heavily on library management features, but user interviews revealed that readers craved a sense of place and personal connection to their digital shelves. This insight shifted the design direction toward a more atmospheric, cozy aesthetic — using warm tones, soft textures, and subtle motion to evoke the calm of a quiet reading nook. Usability testing underscored the importance of balancing aesthetic charm with clarity. While the visual theme invited users in, ensuring accessibility and easy navigation kept them immersed. Iterating on typography, color contrast, and hierarchy helped create an experience that was both inviting and usable.

One key takeaway was that designing for emotional resonance requires empathy at every stage — from research to micro-interactions. Small details, like personalized reading recommendations or the animation of a “page turn,” became powerful ways to make users feel connected to their digital library. If I were to continue developing BookNest, I’d love to explore community features — such as book clubs or shared reading rooms — to extend the feeling of coziness into a social dimension. Additionally, integrating features for mindful reading habits, like focus modes or reading reflections, could deepen user engagement over time. Ultimately, BookNest reminded me that good design goes beyond usability; it’s about crafting experiences that resonate emotionally. Creating a cozy digital library isn’t just about storing books — it’s about nurturing a space where stories, memories, and moments of calm can thrive.
